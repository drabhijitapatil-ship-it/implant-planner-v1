<analysis>**original_problem_statement:**
The user wants to build a mobile app using Expo for the Department of Prosthodontics to plan and manage dental implant procedures.

**PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS (as evolved through conversation):**

1.  **User Roles & Access Control:**
    *   **Postgraduate (PG) Student:** Can add new patient procedures, fill out checklists, and view their own procedures.
    *   **Supervisor (formerly Instructor):** Can view/edit/approve procedures they are assigned to.
    *   **Implant Incharge:** Can view/edit/approve all procedures.
    *   **Administrator:** Has full access, including managing users. (Dr. Abhijit Patil holds this role).
    *   **Nurse:** Read-only access to a dashboard of approved, scheduled patients.

2.  **Core Workflow (Phase-Based):**
    *   A PG student creates a new procedure, filling patient details, selecting a Supervisor and Implant Incharge, and completing the Pre-Surgical Protocol checklist.
    *   The submission is sent to the assigned Supervisor and Implant Incharge for dual approval.
    *   If the Supervisor and Implant Incharge are the same person (e.g., Dr. Abhijit Patil), one approval counts for both.
    *   Once Phase 1 is approved, the Surgical Protocol (Phase 2) becomes active.
    *   The student completes the Phase 2 checklist post-surgery and submits it for final approval from the same Supervisor and Implant Incharge.
    *   A fully approved procedure is marked as Stage 1 Implant Placement Done Successfully.

3.  **Features:**
    *   **Data Entry Form:** A comprehensive form for new procedures with a calendar date-picker, fixed time slots, and styled dropdowns for selecting faculty. Mandatory fields are highlighted in red if left empty.
    *   **Scheduling Restrictions:** Students cannot schedule appointments less than 24 hours in advance. Saturdays have only one slot at 9:30 AM. Sundays are blocked.
    *   **Profile Photos:** Users can upload a profile photograph.
    *   **Notifications:** A notification is sent to a Supervisor when they are assigned to a new procedure.
    *   **UI/UX:**
        *   A persistent bottom tab bar for main navigation.
        *   A Back to Dashboard button is present on all inner screens, positioned at the bottom-left.
        *   Specific color-coding for action buttons (e.g., active Phase 2 button is green, PDF export is red).
    *   **User Management:** Administrators and Implant Incharges have the ability to add and remove users via API endpoints.
    *   **PDF Export:** A prominent Export as PDF button appears at the bottom of the screen for fully completed and approved procedures.

**User's preferred language**: English
